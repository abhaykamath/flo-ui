# Flo — Manifesto

> "Good software should be a gift, not a trap."

---

## What Flo Is

Flo is a financial tracker. Simple on the surface, solid underneath. It helps people know where their money goes — not through anxiety, dark patterns, or AI-powered insights that tell you coffee is expensive. Through calm, honest visibility and the quiet discipline of a daily habit.

Flo is free. Properly free. No trial. No freemium tier. No features locked behind a paywall. The person who can't afford a subscription is often the person who needs a money tracker most. We remember that.

---

## Why It Exists

Every other tracker wants something from you. A subscription. Your bank credentials. A partnership with the brands you're trying to spend less on. Or they start simple and quietly become bloated, slow, and desperate for your upgrade.

Flo is a quiet act of rebellion. Built by one person with too many spreadsheets and too little patience for the above.

---

## Who It's For

Flo is for anyone, anywhere in the world, who wants to understand their money without being talked down to, upsold, or surveilled.

- The person who just wants to log expenses and see a monthly total.
- The person who wants budgets, goals, categories, and a full breakdown.
- The person who trusts nothing and wants their data in their own Google Sheet.
- The person who just wants it to work and doesn't care where it lives.

All of these people are welcome. None of them are more important than the others.

---

## What Flo Is Not

- Flo is not a banking app. We don't touch your accounts.
- Flo is not a budgeting coach. We show you the data. You decide what to do with it.
- Flo is not a subscription business pretending to be a product.
- Flo is not trying to become the next Mint, YNAB, or anything else. It's trying to be Flo.

---

## The Core Values

### Freedom
Your data belongs to you. You choose where it lives — your Google Sheet or our cloud. You can export it anytime, in any format, and walk away. Zero lock-in. We are comfortable with that.

### Discipline
The hardest part of managing money isn't knowing what to do. It's showing up consistently. The streak system exists to reward the habit of logging, not the perfection of your finances.

### Calm
Flo should feel like a notebook, not a dashboard. Warm, quiet, intentional. No red alerts. No aggressive nudges. No dark patterns designed to make you feel bad so you'll upgrade.

### Craft
Every screen, every interaction, every line of copy was thought about. Flo is not assembled — it is made. You should be able to feel the difference.

### Honesty
No hidden fees. No gotchas. No "we updated our privacy policy" emails that mean something changed. What Flo says it does, it does.

### Passion
This exists because someone cared enough to build it. That energy should be visible in the product. If it ever stops feeling like it was made with love, something has gone wrong.

---

## The Design Philosophy

Flo looks like a notebook someone actually uses. Paper. Craft. Ruled lines. A little worn at the edges. Warm pastels, not tech-company gradients. Organic shapes. Drawn, not generated.

The typography is confident and warm — not corporate. The copy has a sense of humour. The app is pleasant to use. These are not accidents.

**Paper. Pastel. Craft. Calm. Indie. Honest. A little funny.**

---

## On Revenue

Flo has a voluntary contribution model. If it makes your life better and you feel like saying thanks, you can buy the developer a coffee. That's it. There are no tiers. There is no pressure. There is no badge that says "Supporter" that makes you feel guilty for not having it.

This is sustainable because the app is maintained in the age of AI-assisted development, which dramatically lowers the cost of keeping good software alive. The goal is not to become a company. The goal is to remain a gift.

---

## The Promise

Flo will:
- Always be free for core functionality
- Always let you export your data
- Never sell your data
- Never add a paywall to features that exist today
- Be maintained with care for as long as it matters
